Airport Navigation Research

Navigating through airports has been a problem for travelers. This research project looks at how passengers interact with space at airports, and proposes a design solution to improve their traveling experience. The research includes: literary reviews, studies, expert insights, personas, surveys, shadowing methods, venn diagrams, ideations, etc.

Literary Review and Research

Personas and Ideations

Literature reviews with Colin Edgard, a psychologist at University of Waterloo, provide a better understanding of how the brain works in way-finding, how much action human takes to get from point A to point B, including both physical and emotional influence. I interviewed Chris Birch – Customer Service Manager and former Guest Experience Director at San Francisco International (SFO) airport, for better insights in the industry. Applying what I’ve research and the industry insights, I narrowed my subject location to SFO. With various method like shadowing a targeted traveler, studying the non-sterile area layout of the airport, I’ve found out that the airport’s industrial interior structure plays a big role in wayfinding, and travelers’ confusion. From high-tech solutions like using Google Glass to a hologram map which is voice interacted, the proposed idea that stood out is changing the look and feel of the airport interior. Instead of adding more signs and direction, I emiliminate them all.
